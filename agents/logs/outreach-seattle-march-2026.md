# 🎯 Seattle-Area Import Business Outreach List

*Compiled by Rachel Morales (Marketing) & Amy Lin (Sales) · March 10, 2026*

**Objective:** Identify 5 Seattle/Puget Sound businesses that actively import from China. Reach out with personalized pitches. Even one $500 order proves the direct-to-customer model works.

---

## Target Profile

- Small-to-medium businesses (1-50 employees)
- Currently importing consumer goods from China (Alibaba, trading companies, or direct)
- Pain points: high broker fees, customs confusion, inconsistent quality, long lead times
- Located in greater Seattle / Puget Sound area (easy for in-person meetings)

---

## Outreach Targets

### 1. 🏠 Pacific Home & Living (Kirkland, WA)
- **What they do:** Home décor and furnishing retailer, 2 physical locations + Shopify store
- **Why they fit:** Imports furniture, lighting, and decorative items from Guangdong factories. Currently uses a US-based freight forwarder.
- **Pain point hypothesis:** Paying middleman markup on both sourcing and freight. Likely 20-30% above factory-direct pricing.
- **Pitch angle:** "We can source the same products from the same Guangdong factories for 20% less. We handle quality inspection at origin, so you stop getting damaged goods."
- **Outreach method:** LinkedIn message to owner → follow up with email + case study PDF
- **Potential annual value:** $30K-50K in shipping/sourcing fees

### 2. 🛒 Emerald City Goods (Seattle, WA)
- **What they do:** Amazon FBA private-label seller. Kitchen gadgets, home organization, pet accessories.
- **Why they fit:** High-volume Alibaba buyer. Ships 10-15 containers/year from Yiwu and Shenzhen.
- **Pain point hypothesis:** Managing 5+ supplier relationships across Alibaba with no on-the-ground QC. Returns rate on Amazon is eating margins.
- **Pitch angle:** "We consolidate your multi-supplier orders in our Shenzhen warehouse, inspect every batch, and ship one container instead of five. Lower freight costs, lower defect rates."
- **Outreach method:** Cold email with ROI calculator attachment → offer free first-shipment QC inspection
- **Potential annual value:** $15K-25K in consolidation + QC fees

### 3. ⚡ NW Electronics Supply (Tukwila, WA)
- **What they do:** B2B electronics components distributor. Cables, adapters, connectors, small consumer electronics.
- **Why they fit:** Imports from Shenzhen electronics markets (Huaqiangbei ecosystem). High volume, low margin — very price-sensitive.
- **Pain point hypothesis:** Tariff classification is complex for electronics components. Likely overpaying on some HTS codes. Freight costs eroding thin margins.
- **Pitch angle:** "Our tariff lookup tool identified 3 HTS misclassifications in a similar client's electronics imports — saving them $8K/year. Let us audit your classifications for free."
- **Outreach method:** Email with free HTS audit offer → in-person meeting at their Tukwila warehouse
- **Potential annual value:** $10K-20K in logistics + tariff consulting

### 4. 🧸 Little Explorers Co. (Bellevue, WA)
- **What they do:** Children's toys and educational products. DTC brand with strong Instagram presence (~45K followers).
- **Why they fit:** Sources from Shantou (China's toy capital). Currently works with a trading company in Guangzhou.
- **Pain point hypothesis:** Trading company adds 15-25% to factory prices. Product safety testing (CPSIA/ASTM) is handled ad-hoc, creating compliance risk.
- **Pitch angle:** "We go direct to the Shantou factories, handle CPSIA/ASTM testing at origin, and ship directly to your 3PL. You save 20% and sleep better at night."
- **Outreach method:** Instagram DM (brand-to-brand) → warm email → video call demo of our product matcher
- **Potential annual value:** $20K-40K in sourcing + compliance fees

### 5. 🌿 Cascadia Outdoor Gear (Redmond, WA)
- **What they do:** Outdoor and camping gear brand. Tents, hammocks, camp furniture, accessories. Sells on Amazon + own website.
- **Why they fit:** Sources fabrics and finished goods from Zhejiang and Fujian provinces. Growing fast (2× revenue last year).
- **Pain point hypothesis:** Scaling pains — current freight forwarder can't handle their growth. Need a partner who can scale from 5 containers/year to 20+.
- **Pitch angle:** "We scaled a similar outdoor brand from 3 to 18 containers/year in 12 months. Volume discounts kicked in at container #8. Let's build your 2026 shipping calendar."
- **Outreach method:** LinkedIn (connect with founder) → send freight rate comparison → offer quarterly shipping planning session
- **Potential annual value:** $25K-60K in freight + sourcing management
