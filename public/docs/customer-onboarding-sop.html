1. Purpose

This SOP defines the standard process for onboarding new customers from initial contact through first successful delivery. All team members must follow this procedure to ensure consistent, professional service.

Every new customer interaction starts with Alex + Jacky in the loop. Tiffany handles day-to-day follow-up AFTER the initial handoff is approved.

3. Detailed Procedures

Stage 1: Lead Intake

1Capture the lead

  • Log in ContactInquiry table (auto if via website form)
  • Record: name, email, phone, company, product interest, source
  • Assign status: "new"
  • Send Slack/chat notification to @alex and @jacky within 30 minutes
  • Send auto-acknowledgment email within 2 hours
NEVER send a quote or pricing to a new customer without Alex or Jacky's approval first.

Stage 2: Qualification

2Alex qualifies the lead

  • Is this a warm lead (Jacky referral) or cold?
  • What products are they looking to import?
  • Estimated order value ($500 minimum for trial)
  • Timeline urgency (immediate vs. exploring)
  • Are they an existing business or new to importing?
  • Update status to "qualified" or "not qualified"

Stage 3: Free Consultation

3Discovery call with customer

  • Schedule 30-min call (Jacky or Alex leads for first customer)
  • Understand exact product needs, quantities, timeline
  • Explain our service: sourcing → QC → shipping → customs → delivery
  • Emphasize: consultation is FREE, no commitment
  • Collect product URLs, photos, or descriptions for sourcing
  • Send follow-up email within 24 hours summarizing the call
Pricing model: All consulting is FREE. Revenue is earned through shipping margins and service fees embedded in the product + shipping cost. Never charge a separate consulting fee.

Stage 4: Quote Preparation

4Build the quote

  • Research product pricing from 2-3 Chinese factories
  • Calculate shipping cost using our rate engine (CBM × zone rate)
  • Estimate customs duties using HS code + Section 301 rate
  • Include: product cost + shipping + insurance (optional) + duties
  • Apply our margin (embedded in shipping, not shown separately)
  • Get Amy to review financials (margin must be ≥45%)
  • Get Alex to approve before sending

Stage 5: Quote Delivery

5Send and follow up

  • Send quote via system email (uses branded template)
  • Include: itemized costs, shipping timeline, payment terms
  • Quote valid for 30 days
  • Follow up at Day 3 and Day 7 if no response
  • If customer has questions, respond within 2 hours (business hours)

Stage 6: Deposit & Order Conversion

6Collect payment and create order

  • Customer accepts quote → generate payment link via Airwallex
  • Collect 70% deposit (30% balance due before delivery)
  • Convert quote to order in system
  • Send order confirmation email with tracking dashboard link
  • Amy records payment and updates financial records

Stage 7: Sourcing & Shipment

7Execute the order

  • Place order with factory
  • Schedule QC inspection (free for orders >$10K)
  • Send inspection photos/video to customer for approval
  • Arrange shipping (LCL or FCL based on volume)
  • Provide vessel name, departure date, and ETA
  • Send weekly status updates to customer

Stage 8: Delivery & Close

8Complete delivery and collect feedback

  • Notify customer when shipment clears customs
  • Arrange last-mile delivery or warehouse pickup
  • Collect remaining 30% payment
  • Send delivery confirmation email
  • Request feedback / testimonial (for case studies page)
  • Ask about next shipment needs
  • Update CRM status to "closed"
